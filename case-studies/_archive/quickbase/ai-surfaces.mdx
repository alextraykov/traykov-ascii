## Summary

Before Pave, several Quickbase AI surfaces sharpened the same product instinct:

> A designed failure recovers. An undesigned one strands the user.

This case is intentionally lighter than Environments, Pipelines, or Connection Central. The archive supports design-complete and concept evidence more than shipped adoption.

## Surfaces

### AI Product Help

The work framed answer states beyond the happy path: loading, no answer, usage limit, timeout, and recovery.

The point was not to make AI sound confident. It was to tell the user what happened and what they can do next.

### QB AI Channels

Channels work reused connection thinking from Pipelines: the hard moment is not "ask AI." It is when the user authorizes a system and expects the product to respect the boundary.

The connect pattern needed to make source, permissions, and data movement visible enough for a business builder to trust.

### AI Console concepts

Console concepts explored how builders might inspect, configure, and reason about AI behavior at the platform level.

The strongest parts here feed later work rather than stand alone: state matrices, failure handling, permission posture, and recovery paths.

## What I would claim

I would claim design direction and reusable product judgment:

- AI answer states should include failure and limit states from the start.
- Connection boundaries are trust boundaries.
- Recovery is part of the feature, not an error appendix.

I would not claim broad shipment or adoption from this archive. The stronger measured AI evidence belongs to the Pipelines AI creation flow.
