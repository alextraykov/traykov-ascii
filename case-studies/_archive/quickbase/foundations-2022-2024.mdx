## Summary

Nobody hands you a domain.

This case frames the work from 2022 through 2024 that made the later Quickbase stories possible: Environments, Connection Central v2, Pipelines, and the broader platform governance work.

The through-line is application lifecycle for people who are not developers.

## Solutions Hub

I came into the domain through Solutions Hub: research, details pages, accessibility, grid selection, versioning and rollback, Pipelines integration, and failure-state work.

The hard proof from the archive:

- ALM / Solutions Hub grid restyle shipped to ROKS on December 15, 2022.
- I owned research and design tickets around Solutions Hub details, accessibility, versioning, rollback, and failure states.
- The work treated lifecycle safety as a business-builder problem, not only a pro-developer feature.

## Failure states matter

One early design question was simple and load-bearing: what should the UI show when creation fails?

That became UX-1919. It covered async polling, failed status, recovery, disabled actions, and whether a user could build on top of a failed version.

Designing the error case is where lifecycle tools earn trust.

![Version details with restore-failed state.](/case-studies/quickbase/foundations-restore-failed.webp)

## Sandboxes -> Environments

In 2023, the problem was Sandboxes and Merge Map: how changes made in a safe copy of an app reconcile back into the live app.

The concept did not fully ship then. The organization was not ready to invest in the hardest part.

That did not make it wasted work. The same conceptual problem matured into the 2026 Environments story: Dev -> Test -> Prod, review gates, deployment safety, version state, and governance.

## Feature retirement

In 2024 I wrote a UX plan to retire Automations, Webhooks, and Actions and move users forward into Pipelines.

Retirement design is unglamorous platform work. It means communication, migration support, phased limits, feedback loops, and enough empathy to move people without pretending the old thing never mattered.

## Team practice

The foundations were not only features.

In 2023 I authored a design methodology for ALM Business Builder work: jobs-to-be-done, Eisenhower-style prioritization, Nielsen heuristics, and POUR accessibility.

I also created the Innovation Weekly Critique practice, a precursor to later recorded critique.

## What this proves

The later portfolio pieces look cleaner because the domain got earned here.

The honest version is not "I had a vision and everyone followed it." It is that repeated research, QA, accessibility, failure modeling, and sunset work built enough credibility to own bigger platform problems later.
