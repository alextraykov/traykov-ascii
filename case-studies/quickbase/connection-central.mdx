## Summary

Connection Central is where Quickbase admins understand what systems, connections, tables, and pipelines are touching company data.

One boundary matters: v1 was Johnny Lee's design, not mine. My case is v2: the 2024 research-led rebuild of the information architecture, interaction model, and launch scope.

The strongest thing on this project was not a screen. It was the set of good ideas I cut so the right structure could ship.

## Project frame

- Role: Lead Product Designer.
- Scope: research, IA, interaction design, MVP definition, Pendo strategy.
- Team: PM Stasha Markovska, engineering lead Dimitar Arnaudov, co-research with Lisa Sawyer.
- Launch: GA on December 17, 2024.

## Problem

Connection Central v1 was informative but sprawling. Builders could see things. They could not reliably reason about what was wired to what.

Before drawing a new structure, I ran a Structured Association survey with 13 Solution Architects. They were the closest proxy we had for power builders living inside integrations every day.

The finding was consistent: people think in terms of resources and impact, not internal object plumbing.

## IA decision: lead with resources

The core IA move was resources-first. The surface needed to show what a connected system exposes, where those resources appear, and which pipelines depend on them.

![Connection Central v2 resources-first IA flow.](/case-studies/quickbase/cc-v2-ia-flow.webp)

The two-pane model kept context while letting admins inspect detail. A control room stops working when the user loses the map just to read one dial.

![Two-pane resources detail model.](/case-studies/quickbase/cc-resources-detail-two-pane.webp)

## Scope decision: protect the launch shape

The survey generated more good ideas than one release could hold. Engineering had a hard GA date and limited UI capacity late in the chain.

I removed richer accordion and popover detail from the MVP on July 22, 2024. The idea was not wrong. It was not load-bearing enough for this launch.

That cut preserved the part that would be expensive to change later: the resources-first IA and control-room shape.

![Pipelines tab in the Connection Central hub.](/case-studies/quickbase/cc-pipelines-list.webp)

## Measurement plan

There was no beta. On November 6, 2024, I aligned with the PM to go straight to GA to hit the H2 date.

That made post-launch measurement more important, so I authored the v2 Pendo strategy: new-feature guide, onboarding walkthrough, feedback poll, and help update.

The archive confirms:

- GA on December 17, 2024.
- Research-led IA grounded in n=13 Solution Architect survey.
- Follow-up backlog tied to deferred survey needs.

The archive does not include Pendo adoption numbers for Connection Central v2. I would not claim adoption without that export.

## What I would do differently

I would still make the no-beta call for the date. I would fight harder for five moderated pre-GA sessions with the same Solution Architects who took the survey.

Pendo can tell what happened after launch. It cannot tell why a builder hesitated on a resource filter or misread a status.
