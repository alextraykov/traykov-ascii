## Summary

This is the management and operating-system story behind the product work.

Inside Quickbase I work across Core and AWE/Pave, with two design systems, distributed teams, and product requests arriving from multiple directions. The job is not only to produce my own screens. It is to keep the right work moving, help designers make stronger decisions, and make the design team easier to work with.

Some evidence is confidential. I am keeping review contents, candidate details, private people feedback, internal URLs, and names out of the public page.

- Scope: Core platform work plus AWE / Pave.

## Cross-BU design intake

Cross-BU requests can easily become Slack negotiation. I owned a central lead-level intake so requests could be sequenced, rejected, redirected, or staffed without every designer renegotiating priorities alone.

The strongest source-backed model is the Connection Central v2 Feature Design Tracker. It used request type, priority, design status, decision date, and follow-up state to keep design work visible. That matters because a tracker is not documentation after the fact; it is a decision surface.

The operating rule was simple:

- Feature-level work routes through intake.
- Fit-and-finish work moves onto Jira stories.
- Priority conflicts route through design leadership instead of forcing individual designers to drop half-built work because the loudest request arrived last.

## Design operating standards

The ALM Business Builder design process plan is strong leadership evidence because it shows process definition, not only request handling.

It set expectations around:

- jobs-to-be-done framing
- research-risk and prioritization
- milestone setting
- deliverable expectations
- accessibility checks
- design-system reference
- prototyping
- specs
- testing

This is the kind of operating standard that makes later product work easier to review. It gives the team a shared way to decide whether a design is ready, instead of treating quality as taste.

## Recorded async critique

I built critique practice for a team split across time zones. The goal was to stop treating people in other time zones as second-class participants.

The 2023 Innovation Weekly Critique ritual had prep expectations, async fallback in `#ux-critique`, and guidance to keep discussion visible instead of moving decisions into DMs. Later Teams evidence supports recorded critiques so designers could review work asynchronously.

The current practice has three modes:

- Fast Pass.
- Domain Share.
- Formal critique.

The important point is not that critiques existed. It is that critique became an operating system: pre-read, visible questions, async participation, and a stronger record of why decisions were made.

## Hiring and reviews

As UX Team Lead, I owned open design requisitions, the Senior Product Designer funnel, the take-home assignment, candidate coordination, panel feedback, and candidate experience.

One concrete example from the M365 hiring evidence: I filed `SUPPORT-18624` to unblock a candidate issue. That is small, but it shows the work was not only evaluation. Candidate experience was part of the ownership surface.

Review evidence stays deliberately conservative. The archive supports review artifacts and annual-review calendar evidence, but the contents are sensitive and not reproduced.

## Weekly rhythm

The archive includes more than 15 substantial weekly updates across 2025 and 2026. Those updates tied work back to Figma, PRDs, Jira epics, and domain priorities.

That sounds small until it is missing. Distributed teams need a visible operating rhythm or everything important becomes oral history.

## What this proves

The leadership case is about multiplying other people, not appearing important.

Good operating systems make hard choices visible:

- what work is actually in flight
- who owns it
- what the review path is
- which requests are out of scope
- how new designers ramp without private context
- how candidate and review processes stay human without leaking confidential detail

## Gaps

This story has strong structural evidence but weak measured throughput evidence. I would like to add ramp-time, review-cycle, intake-resolution, or designer-sentiment measures before treating it as fully proven.
